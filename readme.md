# Brazilian E-Commerce Performance Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes the Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist using PostgreSQL to uncover insights into revenue performance, customer behavior, logistics efficiency, seller activity, and customer satisfaction.

The goal of this project was to understand how OLIST has been performing across the years:
- Designing a relational database
- Writing analytical SQL queries
- Performing exploratory data analysis
- Generating business insights
- Creating presentation-ready visualizations

---

# Business Problem
Olist is a Brazilian e-commerce marketplace that connects sellers to customers across Brazil. 

This analysis aims to answer key business questions such as:
- Which states generate the most revenue?
- Which product categories perform best?
- How do delivery delays affect customer satisfaction?
- What payment methods do customers prefer?
- How concentrated are sellers geographically?
- What operational risks exist within the marketplace?

# Key Business Insights

## Revenue Concentration
Most revenue is generated from southeastern Brazilian states, especially São Paulo, showing a strong regional concentration.

## Product Demand
Home and lifestyle categories such as Bed Bath Table and Furniture generated the highest sales activity.

## Customer Satisfaction
Customer satisfaction is more strongly affected by delivery speed than shipping cost.

## Logistics Performance
Late deliveries negatively impact review scores and overall customer experience.

## Seller Distribution Risk
A large percentage of sellers are concentrated in São Paulo, creating operational dependency on a single region.

## Payment Behavior
Credit cards dominate customer payment preferences, while installment payments (2-12) are widely used.
